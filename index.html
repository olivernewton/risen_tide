What's Biting

What You Can Catch

The inshore waters around St. Marys, Cumberland Island, and Camden County offer fantastic opportunities to target a variety of prized coastal species year-round.

Redfish
The top target in Georgia waters. Aggressive fighters found in shallow flats and tidal creeks.
Speckled Trout
Seasonal favorites found in grass flats and marsh edges. Great table fare.
Flounder
Ambush predators that lie flat on the bottom. A prized and delicious catch.
Sheepshead
Hard fighters around structure. Known for stealing bait — they require real skill to hook.
Black Drum
Powerful fish found near oyster bars and structure. Can run large inshore.
Whiting
Consistent fighters great for beginners and families. Found in sandy inshore bottoms.

Captain's Note: Fishing conditions, target species, and regulations vary by season, tide, and weather. Captain Andrew will always guide the trip based on what's biting and what gives your group the best experience on the water.

Conditions That Make Great Fishing

The inshore waters of Camden County and Cumberland Island are heavily influenced by tides, wind, and seasonal patterns. Captain Andrew monitors conditions daily and will always recommend the best trip based on what the water is doing.

🌅
Early Morning = Best Action Redfish and trout are most active in the early morning hours on incoming tides.
🌊
Tide Matters Most Tidal movement is the #1 factor in inshore fishing success. Captain Andrew times trips to maximize tidal windows.
💨
Wind Under 15 mph Light winds make for the best casting and most comfortable boat ride on Georgia's inshore waters.
📅
Year-Round Fishing Georgia's mild climate means great inshore fishing in every season — species and tactics just shift.
